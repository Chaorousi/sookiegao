From Cause to Care: Rethinking Responsibility for Stray Animal Welfare

Sookie Gao · Research writing · ~2,400 words

“The greatness of a nation and its moral progress can be judged by the way its animals are treated,” said by Mahatma Gandhi. While this statement is frequently cited in discussions of animal welfare, it raises a difficult question: who, exactly, is responsible for ensuring that animals are treated ethically?

In modern society, the mistreatment of animals exists across many contexts from industrial farming to urban neglect. Among these, the most visible issues are the treatment of stray animals. Stray animal populations are not a natural phenomenon, they are the cumulative result of human choices, economic structures, and regulatory failures.

A key issue in protecting stray animals is deciding who has the most power to make change: individuals, corporations, or governments, and whether animal welfare should be something enforced by rules or depend on people’s goodwill.
